Level-1
Personnel

  • Firefighters: Trained professionals responsible for extinguishing fires, rescuing people and animals, and providing emergency medical assistance.
  • Paramedics and Emergency Medical Technicians (EMTs): Personnel trained to provide pre-hospital medical care, including assessing and treating injuries, managing medical emergencies, and administering medications.
  • Hazardous Materials (Hazmat) Specialists: Experts trained to handle and mitigate incidents involving hazardous substances, such as chemical spills or leaks.
  • Technical Rescue Specialists: Personnel trained in specialized rescue techniques for incidents such as confined space rescues, high-angle rescues, trench rescues, and water rescues.
  • Incident Commanders: Experienced personnel responsible for managing and coordinating emergency response operations, making strategic decisions, and ensuring the safety of personnel on scene.

level -2
Equipment

  • Firefighting Apparatus: This includes fire engines, ladder trucks, rescue trucks, and specialized vehicles equipped with firefighting equipment such as hoses, nozzles, pumps, and water tanks.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Protective gear worn by firefighters and rescue personnel, including turnout gear (fire-resistant jackets and pants), helmets, gloves, boots, and self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) for respiratory protection.
  • Rescue Equipment: Tools and gear used for extricating individuals from vehicles, buildings, or other confined spaces, such as hydraulic rescue tools (e.g., spreaders, cutters), ropes and rigging equipment, and lifting devices.
  • Hazardous Materials (Hazmat) Gear: Protective suits, respirators, and decontamination equipment used by Hazmat specialists to safely handle and mitigate incidents involving hazardous substances.
  • Medical Equipment: Supplies and devices used by paramedics and EMTs to assess and treat injuries and medical emergencies, including defibrillators, medical kits, oxygen tanks, and immobilization devices.

Allocation


level-3
Vehicles

  • Fire Engines (Pumpers): Equipped with water tanks, pumps, hoses, and other firefighting equipment for extinguishing fires and providing water supply at the scene.
  • Aerial Ladder Trucks: Vehicles with extendable ladders and platforms for accessing elevated areas, conducting rescue operations, and providing elevated water streams during firefighting operations.
  • Rescue Trucks: Vehicles equipped with specialized rescue equipment and tools for extrication, technical rescues, and hazardous materials incidents.
  • Ambulances: Vehicles equipped to transport patients to medical facilities, staffed by paramedics or EMTs capable of providing emergency medical care en route.
